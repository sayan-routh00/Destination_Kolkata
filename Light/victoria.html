Victoria Memorial

The Victoria Memorial, set in sprawling landscaped gardens on the Maidan, is Kolkata’s most famous landmark — an imposing white-marble complex and museum built between 1906–1921 in memory of Queen Victoria.

Why visit: Exceptional architecture combining Indo-Saracenic and European classical styles, a rich collection of paintings, sculptures, rare manuscripts, arms and portraits that narrate the colonial and cultural history of the city and wider region. The gardens are a city favourite for morning and evening walks.

History & Architecture

Conceived by Lord Curzon shortly after Queen Victoria's death in 1901, the memorial was designed by Sir William Emerson in an Indo-Saracenic revivalist style with Italianate details. Construction ran from 1906 and the building was completed in 1921. The building is crafted from Makrana marble and features a large central dome crowned by the Angel of Victory. Inside, there are portrait halls, a sculpture gallery, arms & armour rooms, and the Kolkata Gallery which interprets the city's history.

Visitor practicals

  • Opening hours (Galleries): 10:00 AM – 6:00 PM. The galleries remain closed on Mondays and on designated national holidays (Republic Day, Independence Day, Mahatma Gandhi’s Birthday, Dussehra, Christmas, Holi, Eid — check official calendar).
  • Gardens hours: Generally open from early morning to early evening (approx. 6:00 AM – 6:00 PM) — gardens may have separate park timings.
  • Suggested visit duration: 1.5–2.5 hours to explore main galleries and stroll the grounds.
  • Accessibility: Ramps and accessible routes exist but check with the visitor desk for wheelchair assistance.

Tickets & Booking

  • Gallery Ticket (official rates): Indian Nationals ₹50 per head; Nationals of SAARC countries ₹100 per head; Foreign nationals higher (tourist rates often ₹500; check official site). Concessions and group rates apply — always confirm current rates on the official site.
  • Where to buy: Online via the Victoria Memorial official site or offline from the North/South Gate ticket counters.
  • Official site: victoriamemorial-cal.org

Allowed / Restricted items

  • Photography (non-commercial) and mobile phones: typically allowed for personal use inside galleries (no flash in sensitive exhibits) — always respect signage.
  • Large backpacks and luggage may be restricted; security checks at entry. Food and beverages are not permitted inside galleries.
  • Pets are not allowed in museum galleries (guide/service animals excepted) and must follow garden rules.

Upcoming Events

The Victoria Memorial hosts rotating exhibitions, cultural programmes and special guided tours. Check the official website for the latest calendar of events.

Visitor tips

  • Arrive early to avoid crowds and enjoy the gardens during the cooler morning hours.
  • Combine your visit with nearby attractions on the Maidan such as St. Paul's Cathedral and the Kolkata Race Course for a half-day itinerary.
  • Wear comfortable shoes — the galleries and grounds involve considerable walking.
