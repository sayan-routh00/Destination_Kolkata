Alipore Zoological Garden (Alipore Zoo)

Established in 1876, Alipore Zoo is one of India’s oldest zoological parks and a longstanding attraction for families, researchers and conservationists.

History & Significance

Founded during the British period, the Alipore Zoological Garden was formally opened to the public in 1876. Over the decades it has hosted important breeding programs and has been a centre for zoological research and education in eastern India. The zoo's architecture includes heritage enclosures, a large aviary and an aquarium, reflecting a mix of Victorian planning and later developments to meet modern animal welfare standards.

Visitor Information

  • Typical hours: 09:00 AM – 05:00 PM (check official site for seasonal adjustments and special closures).
  • Suggested visit time: 2–3 hours for main exhibits, aquarium and bird enclosures.
  • Accessibility: Some pathways are accessible; contact the zoo office for assistance.

Tickets & Booking

  • Online booking: West Bengal Zoo Authority (WBZA) operates e-ticketing for major zoological parks including Alipore — use the official e-ticket portal to reserve slots.
  • Typical entry fees: Vary by age and nationality; check the official WBZA portal for up-to-date charges (concessions for students/children).
  • Official booking link: WBZA e-Ticket Portal

Allowed & Restricted Items

  • Allowed: Cameras for personal use (non-commercial), mobile phones, small water bottles.
  • Restricted: Feeding animals with your own food is prohibited; large backpacks and luggage may be inspected or disallowed. Follow staff instructions.
  • Safety: Keep a safe distance from enclosures; follow signage and do not attempt to cross barriers.
