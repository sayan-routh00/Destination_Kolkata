Indian Museum

The Indian Museum in Kolkata is the oldest and one of the largest museums in India, with encyclopaedic collections covering archaeology, art, anthropology, geology, and zoology.

History & Collections

Founded in 1814, the Indian Museum holds significant collections that include archaeological finds from across the subcontinent, a vast collection of Buddhist and Hindu sculptures, numismatics, a natural history gallery, and an extensive mineral and fossil collection. The museum has played a central role in scholarship and public education for over two centuries.

Visitor practicals

  • Opening hours: Galleries typically open ~10:00 AM – 5:00 PM; often closed on Mondays (confirm exact days/holiday closures on the official site).
  • Suggested visit duration: 2–3 hours to explore major galleries; longer for specialist interests.
  • Accessibility: Some galleries have step access; contact the museum for assistance and guided tours.

Tickets & Booking

  • Entry fees: Ticket categories usually include Indian citizens, students, and foreign nationals — rates and concessions vary; check the museum's official site or ticket counter for current fees.
  • Online booking: The Indian Museum historically used an in-person ticketing system; some exhibitions or group visits may be bookable in advance. Use the museum’s official site or contact number for booking details.
  • Official site: indianmuseumkolkata.org

Allowed / Restricted items

  • Photography for personal use is commonly allowed in many galleries (no flash where prohibited). Always follow signage and staff instructions.
  • Large bags and food may be restricted; security checks at entry are common.
  • Touching exhibits is prohibited; guided handling sessions (if any) will be supervised by staff.

Upcoming Events

The museum occasionally organises special exhibitions, lectures and guided tours. Check the official site for the latest announcements.

Visitor tips

  • Allow time: the museum is extensive — plan what galleries you most want to see.
  • Photography: check signage; avoid flash and disturbing exhibits.
  • Combine visit: Park Street, nearby cafes and other central Kolkata attractions are a short walk away.
