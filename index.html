Quick facts

  • Location: Eastern India, on the east bank of the Hooghly River
  • Population (metro): ~14 million (approx.)
  • Language: Bengali, English, Hindi widely used
  • Climate: Tropical wet-and-dry (hot summers, monsoon, mild winter)

Places to Visit — Quick Picks

Below are some important spots in Kolkata. Click any card for a full page with history, tickets, timings and maps.

Victoria Memorial

A majestic white-marble memorial with galleries, art and landscaped gardens — built in the early 20th century.

Indian Museum

India's oldest museum: archaeology, geology, art and natural history in one place.

Alipore Zoological Garden

Historic zoo with varied animal collections, aquarium and visitor programmes.

Science City

Interactive exhibits, Space Theatre & 3D shows — ideal for family visits.

Birla Industrial & Technological Museum (BITM)

Hands-on science exhibits, workshops and education activities.

Eco Park

Kolkata’s largest urban park with lakes, gardens, cycling tracks and themed zones.

Howrah Bridge

Iconic cantilever bridge across the Hooghly River.

Nicco Park

Family amusement park with rides and attractions.

Park Street

Historic dining and nightlife street.

Marble Palace

A private 19th-century mansion with marble statues, paintings and historical artifacts.

Prinsep Ghat

Riverside promenade & colonial monuments.

Jorasanko Thakur Bari

The ancestral home of Rabindranath Tagore, a cultural landmark.
